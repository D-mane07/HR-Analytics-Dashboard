## üîç Deep Dive Analysis: Understanding Attrition Drivers

### 1. Demographic & Educational Impact on Attrition

* **Gender:** Attrition is higher among **Male (150)** employees compared to Female (87). This could warrant further investigation into gender-specific workplace experiences or opportunities.
* **Age Group:** The **26-35 age bracket** shows the highest attrition, with 116 employees leaving. This suggests challenges in retaining early to mid-career professionals, potentially due to career growth opportunities or compensation.
* **Education Field:** Employees with **Life Sciences (41%)** and **Medical (32%)** backgrounds account for the largest share of attrition, indicating potential talent drain in specialized fields.

### 2. Financial & Tenure-Based Attrition

* **Salary Sensitivity:** Attrition is significantly higher for employees in the **'Upto 5K' salary slab (163 employees)**. As salary increases, attrition drops sharply, underscoring the importance of competitive compensation, especially for lower-income roles.
* **Critical Tenure Point:** The highest attrition occurs around the **2-year mark (59 employees)** at the company. This is a crucial period where employees might evaluate their long-term prospects. Attrition then sharply declines, suggesting that employees who stay past this point are more likely to commit longer-term.

### 3. Role-Specific Attrition & Satisfaction

* **High-Risk Roles:** **Laboratory Technicians (62)** and **Sales Executives (57)** experience the highest attrition counts. This may point to high stress, demanding targets, or limited growth paths within these specific job roles.
* **Job Role Satisfaction:** For Laboratory Technicians, even with a Job Role Satisfaction score of '1' (which might indicate lowest satisfaction), 20 employees still left, highlighting potential deep-seated issues in this department beyond just general satisfaction.

## üöÄ Strategic Recommendations for Retention

1.  **Targeted Retention for Mid-Career Talent:** Develop enhanced career development plans, mentorship programs, and clear growth paths for employees in the **26-35 age group** to address their high attrition rate.
2.  **"2-Year Mark" Intervention:** Implement proactive "stay interviews" or talent reviews for employees approaching their two-year anniversary to identify and address concerns before they consider leaving.
3.  **Compensation Review:** Conduct a market competitiveness review for roles falling into the **'Upto 5K' salary slab**. Increasing compensation or offering better benefits might significantly reduce turnover in these high-attrition segments.
4.  **Specialized Talent Engagement:** Investigate the reasons for high attrition in **Life Sciences** and **Medical** educational backgrounds, potentially through exit interviews or targeted engagement surveys.
